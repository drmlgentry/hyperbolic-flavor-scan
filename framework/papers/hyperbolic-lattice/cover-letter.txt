I am pleased to submit the manuscript "Discrete Weight Spectra and Coordinate Rigidity in Cocompact Hyperbolic Group Actions" for consideration in Geometriae Dedicata.

This paper establishes three structural results for torsion-free cocompact group actions on hyperbolic 3-space. For a group acting properly discontinuously and cocompactly on hyperbolic 3-space, with logarithmic weights assigned via the displacement function, we prove: (1) each boundary direction admits a unique minimal representative in the group; (2) there is a uniform positive spectral gap proportional to the injectivity radius of the quotient manifold between distinct weight values; (3) integer coordinates defined via the Milnor-Svarc quasi-isometry are stable under bounded deformations. All results follow from hyperbolicity, proper discontinuity, and the classification of isometries; no additional hypotheses are required.

This paper is part of a series on discrete geometric structure in compact hyperbolic manifolds. A companion paper, "Geometric Origin of CP Phases from Hyperbolic Holonomy," has been submitted to the Journal of Geometry and Physics; a second companion, "Discrete Mixing Operators from Boundary Sector Geometry," has also been submitted to the Journal of Geometry and Physics. Each paper is mathematically self-contained.

This manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere. I have no conflicts of interest to declare.
